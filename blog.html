Sayonara travel, as we knew it

I'm sure I'm not alone in feeling a little out of control, out of the loop, out of order etc. If not for the global pandemic I would be traveling right now around the Asia-Pacific region. Instead I have had more continuous time in New Zealand than I ever have since moving here from Canberra in 2014

Covid has taken away alot of our choices and control, but it has also given us the gifts of reflection, time with friends and permission to slow down in our daily lives and work.

For most people there will be a change in your lives that you may not have foreseen or planned since March 2020 (five months ago, but feels like decades). I moved into a new apartment, got married, became my own boss, met new friends, vacationed more locally and tapped into my passions more fully.

I have always been a fan of uncovering deals, freebies and perks of memberships. So my new wife and I have really leaned into the benefits for the entertainment book, grabone, rewards points, flybuys, the crypto currency debit card, gift cards etc. It has always worked out much cheaper to eat out than cook at home, but now you have the warm fuzzy feeling of helping an industry that is really hurting. Both my wife and I have worked in hospitality jobs in various forms so since the supermarket oligarchy got all the money during lockdown levels 4 and 3 lockdown it makes sense to go back to the food and bev outlets who couldn't trade during the lockdown.

Also domestic tourism and travel is not only our only option for a while but it doesn't require jet lag or transiting through airports, which I enjoy, but I totally get how for most people it is a burdensome hassle on the way to their all inclusive fiji family resort, or japanese ski vacation.
