    Inland Revenue

    The New Zealand system broadly operates a broad base, low rate system of taxation. Meaning the top marginal rate, regardless of income is 33% and 28% on prescribed investment income. Portability of Australian Superannuation to New Zealand is relatively straightforward.

    • https://www.ird.govt.nz/
    Work and Income

    There is a universal aged pension, accessible after 10 years working age residency in New Zealand, five years of which need to be after the age of 50. The rate (currently approximately $22,000NZD per annum for a single, or $34,000 for a couple combined after tax, this is not means tested) is a key part of the underpinning social welfare system to prevent aged poverty, the fund is stewarded by the Guardians of New Zealand Superannuation.

    Employer Funded Superannuation

    The employer funded superannuation in New Zealand is less than Australian comparisons in part due to this counterbalance. There is also the potential to draw down this pension from a number of New Zealand Realm Pacific Island neighbours.

      https://www.msd.govt.nz/about-msd-and-our-work/newsroom/media-releases/2019/nz-super-or-veterans-pension-now-easier-to-get-in-pacific-countries.html
    Accident Compensation Corporation

    The Accident Compensation Corporation is another benefit in the broad New Zealand landscape which provides financial support and access to medical treatment for a range of accidents and injury. It is also underpinned by a large investment pool stewarded by the corporation to ensure its sustainability.

      https://www.acc.co.nz/about-us/
    New Zealand Parliament

    New Zealand Parliament is also Unicameral meaning that it doesn't not have two chambers of Parliament, only one. However a robust Parliamentary committee system and independent auditor function keeps the system in balance. This means that law passage is often a far more agile and speedy process when it needs to be, compared to Canberra.

    • https://www.parliament.nz
